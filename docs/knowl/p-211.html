First consider a ppav \(X/K\) s.t.

\begin{gather} \knowl{./knowl/eqn-parity-dagger.html}{\text{(1.1)}} \equiv \frac{\prod_v c(X/K_v)}{\prod_v c(Y/K_v)} \frac{\Omega_X}{\Omega_Y} \frac{|\Sha_0(X) \lb p^\infty\rb |}{|\Sha_0(Y) \lb p^\infty\rb |} \pmod{{K^\times}^\vee}\tag{1.2} \end{gather}

  1. Can we compute

    \begin{equation} \frac{\prod_v c(X/K_v)}{\prod_v c(Y/K_v)} \frac{\Omega_X}{\Omega_Y}\text{?}\tag{1.3} \end{equation}

    Leads us to Jacobians of hyperelliptic curves of genus \(g\)

  2. Can we compute

    \begin{equation} \frac{|\Sha_0(X) \lb p^\infty\rb |}{|\Sha_0(Y) \lb p^\infty\rb |}\text{?}\tag{1.4} \end{equation}

    Leads us to Jacobians of hyperelliptic curves of genus \(g\)

  3. Need an isogeny \(\Psi\) of degree \(2^g\) s.t.

    \begin{equation*} \Psi \colon J \to J' \end{equation*}

    i.e. the codomain must be a Jacobian of a hyperelliptic curve otherwise we cannot compute 1. or 2.
